Isabel Baskin; Keynote Speaker
Izzi Baskin graduated from Swarthmore College in 2017 with majors in computer science and sociology. Since college, she has worked as a software engineer in various industries. She last worked at BallotReady where she built software to help people vote informed all the way down the ballot. She is currently a front-end engineer for Dia&Co, a plus size clothing company that offers stylish clothes for plus size women and speaks out against fatphobia in the fashion industry and beyond. Outside of work, Izzi can be found browsing local bookstores and watching gymnastics.



Allison Lettiere; Panelist
Allison is a Master’s student at Stanford University studying computer science, with a focus in artificial intelligence. She is interested in using AI to build assistive and accessible technologies, and will be starting work on Apple’s Accessibility team as a software engineer in the fall.



Elina Rawat; Panelist
Elina Rawat is a freshman at Northwestern University studying Computer Science and Economics. She is Indian, but grew up in Hong Kong, so that is where she calls home. She is a big advocate of Women in STEM and hopes to inspire others to pursue STEM as well.



Julia Fairbank; Panelist
Julia Fairbank is a sophomore at Middlebury College where she studies computer science alongside philosophy. In high school, Julia worked on a computational genetic lab team through Duke University where she built RNA models, analyzed different potential drug docking points, ran thousands of drug candidates against those regions, and compared results to pattern match what makes a success dock. She chose to continue in STEM by majoring in computer science but chose to supplement it with philosophy which allows her to study both hard and soft logical reasoning. Julia will be working for a private equity firm this coming summer, PeakSpan Capital, which partners and invests in growth-stage software companies. She is Chicago raised and loves sailing on the lake.



Deena Tenzer; Workshop Leader
Deena Tenzer is a passionate software engineer with over five years of experience in the technology industry. She currently works at Snapsheet in downtown Chicago where she creates, architects and maintains their transactions platform as part of a small team of three people. Before COVID-19, Deena facilitated the Water Payton Girls Who Code (GWC) club and ran GWC summer immersive programs for high school aged students interested in STEM. Outside of work, Deena enjoys bike riding, figure skating and spending time outside!
